## Overview

A **bias audit** is an independent, impartial evaluation of an automated system to identify whether it produces disparate outcomes across protected demographic groups such as race/ethnicity, gender, or age.

### NYC Local Law 144 Requirements

Under NYC LL 144, employers must:
- Commission an independent bias audit from a qualified third party before using an AEDT
- Repeat the audit annually
- Publicly post a summary of the most recent audit results on their website
- Retain audit results for at least three years

### What Auditors Evaluate

A bias audit typically analyzes:

- **Selection rates** by race/ethnicity, gender, and other protected classes
- **Impact ratio** — the rate at which each demographic group passes screening
- **Adverse impact** — whether differences exceed the 80% rule or statistical significance thresholds
- **False positive/negative rates** by demographic group
